1. Executive Summary

What is CleanCloud?
CleanCloud is a comprehensive Point of Sale (POS) and business management platform designed specifically for laundromats, dry cleaners, alterations, and shoe cleaning businesses. The system provides end-to-end solutions for managing orders, tracking cleaning workflows, processing payments, generating reports, and managing customer relationships.
Who Should Use This System?
  • Laundromat Owners: Manage wash-dry-fold services, self-service machines, and retail products
  • Dry Cleaning Businesses: Track garments through the cleaning process with detailed item tagging
  • Alteration Shops: Manage custom orders with specific customer requirements
  • Multi-Location Operators: Centralize management across multiple stores

Key Business Objectives

Objective Description Business Benefit
Streamline Checkout Fast, intuitive POS interface for quick order processing Reduce customer wait times by up to 50%
Order Lifecycle Tracking Track orders from drop-off to pickup through every stage Eliminate lost items and improve accountability
Multi-Category Management Handle WDF, Retail, Household, and Subscription products Diversify revenue streams in one system
Business Analytics Comprehensive reporting on sales, customers, and operations Data-driven decisions for growth
Hardware Integration Connect printers, scales, scanners, and payment terminals Seamless operations without manual entry
Multi-Store Management Centralized control for multiple locations Scale business efficiently

3. Module 1: Point of Sale (POS)

Purpose:
The POS module is the heart of daily operations. It's the primary interface staff use to create orders, add items, select customers, and process payments. A well-designed POS reduces training time and speeds up customer service.
Key Benefits:
  • Intuitive interface reduces staff training time
  • Quick item selection with visual product buttons
  • Flexible payment options for customer convenience
  • Automatic calculations for pricing, taxes, and discounts

3.1 Feature Overview

The POS module is the primary interface for creating and processing customer orders. It supports multiple product categories, customer management, and various payment methods.

3.2 Product Categories

Why Multiple Categories?
Different product types require different pricing models and workflows. WDF is priced by weight, retail items are per-unit, and subscriptions are recurring. Separating categories ensures accurate pricing and proper workflow routing.
Category Description Pricing Type Use Case
WDF (Wash, Dry, Fold) Laundry services by weight Weight-based ($/lb) Customer drops off a bag of laundry; staff weighs it and charges per pound
Retail Retail products (bags, detergents, softeners) Per item Customer wants to buy a laundry bag or detergent to take home
Household Household items (blankets, pillows, comforters) Per item Customer brings in a comforter or blanket for cleaning
Subscription Products Recurring service plans Subscription-based Regular customer signs up for weekly laundry service at discounted rate

3.3 WDF Section Features

Purpose:
WDF (Wash, Dry, Fold) is the core service for most laundromats. This section allows staff to select service types, record weights, and add special handling instructions.

3.3.1 Service Types

Service Type Description When to Use

Hypoallergenic Gentle detergents for sensitive skin Customers with skin conditions or babies' clothes
Premium High-quality detergents and extra care Customers willing to pay more for better treatment
Standard Regular wash service Default option for most customers
Laundry by Bag Fixed price per bag regardless of weight Promotional offers or simplified pricing

3.3.2 Custom Photo Upload

Why Photo Documentation?
Photos protect both the business and customer by documenting the condition of items before cleaning. This prevents disputes about pre-existing damage and provides visual reference for special handling requirements.
  • Photo documentation capability: Take photos of special items, stains, or damage before cleaning
  • Visual reference: Staff can refer to photos during cleaning to ensure proper treatment
  • Dispute resolution: Photos serve as evidence if customer claims damage occurred during cleaning
